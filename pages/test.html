ERPM? Huh?

Hours of studying, countless sleepless nights, a thousand and one things to study and five long years of toiling away in med school as you prepare to face the dreaded ERPM exam - no pressure right?

But what exactly is the ERPM exam? Why sit for it? Is it as hard as they say it is and how do we prepare for it?

The ERPM exam - more commonly referred to as the nightmare that awaits all innocent foreign med students - is a compulsory exam that anyone who wishes to practice medical care in Sri Lanka needs to sit for. It’s broken down into four sections - the theory examination, clinical examination, oral examination and community medicine and forensics medicine - all of which are focused on the core ERPM subjects. Technically, the ERPM is designed to assess your ability to approach medical practice with the necessary skills or clinical reasoning, intellectual inquiry and decision making and checks your understanding and knowledge of clinical subjects with special focus on medical related issues prevalent in Sri Lanka.

Phew! Stressed? Don’t be. We’re going to take this one step at a time!

ERPM Part A - Theory Examination

Theory examination - Medicine, Paediatrics, Surgery, Psychology and Gynecology & Obstetrics

The theory examination consists of two types of questions

True/False MCQs which tests the candidates’ core knowledge
Single Best Answer (SBA) questions that assess the candidates’ ability to handle and solve basic clinical problems and analyze and understand laboratory results

ERPM Part B - Clinical Examination

The clinical component has 4 subjects with 2 stations per subject

Track 1: Medical Track

  • Medicine
    • Station 1 - Medical Consultation (20 mins)
    • Station 2 - Medical System Examination : 2 short cases (20 mins)
  • Paediatrics
    • Station 3 - Paediatric Consultation (20 mins)
    • Station 4 - Paediatric System Examination : 2 short cases (20 mins)

Track 2: Surgical Track

  • Surgery
    • Station 5 - Surgical Consultation (20 mins)
    • Station 6 - Surgical Spots : 3 short cases (20 mins)
  • Gynecology and Obstetrics
    • Station 7 - Gynecology Consultation and Examination (20 mins)
    • Station 8 - Obstetric Consultation and Examination (20 mins)
